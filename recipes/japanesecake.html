Japanese Cheesecake

Home

Description

Japanese cheesecake also known as souffle-style cheesecake, is a variety of cheesecake known for it's light texture, and more muted sweetness than American style cheesecake. The texture is wobbly and airy when fresh, and a more chiffon-cake like texture once cool. The recipe was first created by Tomotaro Kuzuno after trying a Kasekuchen cheesecake, during a trip to Berlin in the 1960s. It has less calories than a western cheesecake due to containing less creamcheese and sugar.

Recipe

Serves 8

Ingredients

Meringue

  • 5 egg whites
  • 1/4 tsp cream of tartar (lemon juice or vinegar can sub)
  • 1/4 cup (50g) fine suger (gran. not conf.)

Cake Batter

  • 5 egg yolks
  • 8 oz cream cheese
  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 1/2 cup whole milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar (same as for meringue)
  • 2 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1/4 cup flour
  • lemon juice and/or vanilla extract (optional)
  • toppings (optional) may include honey, match, seasonal fruits etc.

Directions

  1. Begin by preheating oven to 330 degrees farenheit.
  2. Begin by preparing your mise-en-place. Make sure to seperate your five eggs. Egg yolks should go in one bowl, and yolks in another. Place directly in respective mixing bowls. Yolks for batter, white for meringue.
  3. Place the butter, cream cheese and sugar for batter into a saucepan. Set on low heat and allow the ingredients to melt, before mixing into a smooth batter
  4. Remove the pot from the heat and add the egg yolks into the batter. Mix well but gently.
  5. Add cornstarch and flour and continue to mix until clumps disappear.
  6. Add milk and any flavors (lemon juice or vanilla)
  7. Mix well. Should result in smooth and clumpless batter. No strain neccesary.
  8. Set aside batter. Add remaing ingredients to meringue bowl. (sugar and acid of choice)
  9. Prepare either an 8 inch or two 6 inch cake pans.
  10. Pour batter over meringue and FOLD the batter into the meringue. DO NOT overmix. Incorporate well.
  11. Bake bain-marie style for 25 minutes. If cake does not rise, raise temp by ten degrees. Once risen proceed to next step.
  12. Open door for about ten seconds SLIGHTLY, and lower temp to 245-265 (depending on whether you had to raise temp) then bake an additional 45 minutes.
  13. After cook time is up turn off oven and allow cake to sit for ten minutes.
  14. Remove from oven and cool for a couple minutes.
  15. Use the plate flip method to remove.
